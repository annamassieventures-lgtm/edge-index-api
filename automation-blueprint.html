Claude API — Report Engine
Section-by-Section

Each of the 15 report sections gets its own Claude API call. Prompt = HD chart data + section-specific instructions + relevant parent intake answers. This structure replicates the original manual depth — Claude focuses on one thing at a time and goes deep.

  • 15 API calls per report (~$0.80–1.20 total cost)
  • Each call: chart data + intake context + section prompt
  • N8N assembles all 15 outputs into HTML
  • Depth preserved = "someone finally understands my child"

Creator Outreach

Automated Email Sequences

Perplexity (or AI web research) builds targeted lists of parenting influencers, child psychology accounts, homeschooling communities, neurodivergent parent advocates, and family wellness creators. Filtered by audience size, engagement, and relevance.

Apollo.io or Instantly.ai sends personalised cold email sequences automatically. Email 1: introduce the report + the "someone finally understands my child" outcome. Email 2: share the creator commission model. Email 3: send a sample report. All tracked, all automated.

Target lists: Parenting bloggers, Instagram mums, child therapists, homeschool educators, NDIS support workers, family coaches, paediatric OTs.

Facebook Group Strategy

Community-Led Distribution

Facebook parenting groups are the highest-converting channel for this product globally. Mums Help Mums, Working Mums, Gentle Parenting, Neurodivergent Parents, Homeschooling — these groups have millions of members and a buying culture around child development tools.

N8N + a social posting tool (Publer or ManyChat) schedules authentic-feeling posts into relevant groups at optimal times. Posts share relatable parenting content that naturally leads to the report. Compliant with group rules — value-first, not sales-first.

Target groups: 50+ parenting Facebook groups globally. Rotated on a schedule. New groups added monthly via Perplexity research.

School & Educator Outreach

B2B Licensing Pipeline

Apollo.io sends automated sequences to primary school principals, learning support coordinators, homeschool co-ops, Montessori schools, and child development centres. The pitch: license the report to offer parents as part of your enrolment or support package.

License model: school pays a monthly fee to offer the report under their brand (white-label). Powered by the same pipeline. This is the highest-value B2B revenue stream — one school = recurring monthly income vs one-off $97 sales.

Instagram & Content Automation

Scheduled Content · No Anna Required

Buffer or Later schedules Instagram content in advance. Content batches are prepared monthly (Anna reviews, approves, done). Reels, carousels, and stories on parenting themes that resonate: "why your child has meltdowns," "how your child really learns," "what your child's behaviour is actually telling you."

Each post ends with a soft CTA to the report. Creators reshare content with their affiliate link. Content compounds over time — every post is an evergreen asset driving ongoing traffic to the checkout.

Children's Intake Form

The Questions That Create the Depth

Adapted from the proven adult intake form. Every question maps directly to a report section — this is what makes the report feel personal rather than generic.

Section 1 · Child's Birth Details
  • Child's first name (for personalisation)
  • Date of birth (day / month / year)
  • Birth time — as accurate as possible (AM/PM)
  • City and country of birth
Section 2 · What You Want to Understand
  • What do you most want this report to help you understand about your child? (select up to 2) — Their learning style / Their emotional patterns / Their strengths & gifts / Their career direction / Why they behave the way they do / Their social energy & friendships
Section 3 · Biggest Parenting Challenges
  • What concerns you most about your child right now? (multi-select) — Emotional meltdowns / School & learning difficulties / Social anxiety / Low confidence / Sensory overwhelm / Focus & attention / Strong will / Anxiety or worry / Feeling misunderstood
  • What's your biggest challenge in parenting this child? (open text)
Section 4 · How They Learn Best
  • How does your child learn best? — Needs to move while learning / Needs quiet focus / Visual & creative / Needs to understand "why" first / Needs lots of encouragement / Learns by doing / Learns by watching others first
  • Does your child struggle at school? (Yes / No / Sometimes)
  • Do they have a diagnosis or suspected neurodivergence? (optional)
Section 5 · Energy & Overwhelm
  • When your child is overwhelmed, they typically: — Have a meltdown / Shutdown & go quiet / Get explosive or angry / Become anxious & clingy / Avoid & escape / Other
  • How quickly do they recover after overwhelm? — Minutes / Hours / A full day / Multiple days
  • What are their main sensory triggers? — Loud noise / Crowds / Unexpected change / Bright lights / Clothing textures / Food textures / Not sure
Section 6 · Social Energy
  • Is your child energised by people or alone time? — Social time / Alone time / Both / Depends on the day
  • How do they handle group settings? — Thrives / Struggles / Depends on who's there
  • Do they struggle with friendships? (Yes / No / Sometimes)
Section 7 · Confidence & Self-Worth
  • Does your child struggle with confidence? (Yes / No / Sometimes)
  • Do they compare themselves to siblings or peers? (Yes / No / Sometimes)
  • Do they know and express their own strengths? (Yes / No / Not yet)
  • How do they typically respond to criticism or failure? — Shutdown / Gets angry / Bounces back quickly / Seeks reassurance / Gives up easily
Section 8 · Daily Support & Rituals
  • How does your child recharge? — Alone time / Creative play / Physical activity / Screen time / Social play / Outdoor/nature time / Sleep / Not sure
  • What time of day is your child most focused and calm? — Morning / Midday / Afternoon / Evening / Varies
  • Is there anything specific you'd love this report to help you with? (free text)

Teen Report — Intake Adjustments

The teen intake uses the same structure but language shifts: "your teen" instead of "your child." Section 2 adds career options: future direction & career, identity & self-worth, peer pressure & social confidence, relationship with parents. Section 4 shifts to: academic performance, exam stress, subject strengths. Section 7 adds: body image, social media impact, comparing to peers. Optional: invite the teen to add their own note at the end — what they'd like understood about themselves.
