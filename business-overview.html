LOW RISK — Technical Core

The HD API works. The report prompts produce the quality that drives "someone finally understands me" responses. The automation tools (N8N, Stripe, Supabase) are mature and well-documented. No technical uncertainty here — just build time.

MEDIUM RISK — PDF Quality

Rendering a luxury 30-page PDF programmatically that matches the quality of a manually produced report is the hardest remaining technical challenge. Puppeteer can do it, but it requires careful HTML template design. Allow extra time here — it determines the product's first impression.

MEDIUM RISK — First License Cold Start

The first community admin relationship has to be earned manually — a conversation, not a vendor pitch. The pitch is strong ("a retention tool disguised as a performance tool") but it requires finding the right admin at the right moment. AI agents can automate the research and initial outreach at scale, but the first 1–2 relationships need genuine relationship energy.

LOW RISK — Market Demand

Trading communities actively spend on tools that give members an edge. 70%+ of traders cite emotional decision-making as their primary loss driver. The trading psychology market is proven, paying, and hungry for anything that gives them an advantage. The Edge Index reframes personal timing as performance intelligence — a language trading communities already speak.

LOW RISK — The Concept Sells Itself

The pitch doesn't require educating a sceptical audience on astrology or Human Design. It simply asks: "what if you knew when your personal decision window was optimal?" Every trader already understands the cost of bad timing. The tool makes that measurable and personal. The conversation is easy — the proof is in the results.

LOW RISK — Competitive Moat

The combination of HD calculation accuracy + quality report prompts + automated delivery pipeline is genuinely hard to replicate quickly. The manual process required deep domain knowledge to build — that same knowledge now powers the automation. Generic AI report competitors can't match the specificity.
