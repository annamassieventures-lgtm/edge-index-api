# The Edge Index — Intelligence Framework

## FRAMING PRINCIPLE
All content is framed as decision behaviour, timing discipline, and performance intelligence.
The underlying mechanics come from Human Design and planetary cycles internally.
The language reads as decision architecture — never astrology.

---

## PART 1 — DECISION ARCHETYPES (Human Design Types)

### 1. Generator
**Decision Pattern:** Momentum builder. Wealth through consistent participation in productive systems.
**Financial Blind Spot:** Over-committing to opportunities that looked good logically but didn't produce sustained engagement.
**Poor Decision Triggers:** Pressure to move quickly, FOMO, investing in things requiring constant forcing.
**Optimal Behaviour:** Respond to clear opportunities, invest where energy stays stable, scale once momentum proves real.
**Power Phrases:** "Sustainable momentum compounds wealth." / "Respond — don't chase." / "Energy alignment precedes financial expansion."

### 2. Manifesting Generator
**Decision Pattern:** Rapid iteration and multi-track execution. Scale what proves traction.
**Financial Blind Spot:** Jumping ahead before testing viability.
**Poor Decision Triggers:** Acting on excitement without validation, over-diversifying, abandoning systems too early.
**Optimal Behaviour:** Test quickly, eliminate failing directions fast, scale only highest-momentum pathways.
**Power Phrases:** "Speed is useful — validation is essential." / "Test fast. Scale what works." / "Momentum reveals the right direction."

### 3. Projector
**Decision Pattern:** Strategic insight and resource optimisation. Wealth through positioning, not volume.
**Financial Blind Spot:** Trying to compete through output rather than insight.
**Poor Decision Triggers:** Chasing unrecognised opportunities, undervaluing strategic contribution, overworking for income.
**Optimal Behaviour:** Wait for recognition of expertise, position insight as premium, structure income through leverage.
**Power Phrases:** "Clarity is my leverage." / "Recognition multiplies value." / "Strategic positioning precedes capital expansion."

### 4. Manifestor
**Decision Pattern:** Initiates systems and market shifts. Wealth from creating new pathways before others see them.
**Financial Blind Spot:** Acting independently without preparing the environment.
**Poor Decision Triggers:** Resistance from others, lack of stakeholder alignment, forcing implementation.
**Optimal Behaviour:** Inform key players before acting, deploy toward new markets, build autonomy into financial structures.
**Power Phrases:** "Initiation creates opportunity." / "Strategic informing unlocks momentum." / "Freedom increases financial scale."

### 5. Reflector
**Decision Pattern:** Environmental evaluator. Detects systemic patterns others miss. Strongest decisions come from observing cycles over time.
**Financial Blind Spot:** Acting too quickly before clarity emerges.
**Poor Decision Triggers:** Urgency, pressure from others, distorting environments.
**Optimal Behaviour:** Observe across full cycles, act after patterns become visible, align capital with stable environments.
**Power Phrases:** "Clarity emerges through observation." / "Environment determines outcome." / "Timing reveals the correct move."

## PART 2 — DECISION AUTHORITIES AND CAPITAL TIMING

### 1. Sacral Authority
**Process:** Immediate gut response.
**When Overridden:** Decisions become mentally forced, energy drops after commitment.
**Timing Pattern:** Fast and binary.
**Capital Rule:** If the internal response is not an immediate yes, the decision is a no.

### 2. Emotional / Solar Plexus Authority
**Process:** Clarity emerges after emotional waves settle.
**When Overridden:** Decisions made at emotional peaks or lows often reverse.
**Timing Pattern:** Cyclical.
**Capital Rule:** Never deploy capital during emotional highs or lows. Wait for the neutral wave.

### 3. Splenic Authority
**Process:** Instant intuitive recognition of risk or safety.
**When Overridden:** Subtle warning signals are ignored.
**Timing Pattern:** Immediate — does not repeat.
**Capital Rule:** Trust the first instinct. It rarely repeats.

### 4. Ego / Heart Authority
**Process:** Decisions driven by willpower and genuine desire.
**When Overridden:** Loss of motivation after commitment.
**Timing Pattern:** Goal-driven.
**Capital Rule:** Deploy capital only toward outcomes you truly want. Never from obligation.

### 5. Self-Projected Authority
**Process:** Clarity comes through speaking decisions out loud.
**When Overridden:** Decisions feel directionless or hollow.
**Timing Pattern:** Reflective.
**Capital Rule:** Verbalise the decision to a trusted person before committing capital.

### 6. Mental / Environmental Authority
**Process:** Clarity emerges through discussion and environment shifts.
**When Overridden:** Mental analysis loops create indecision.
**Timing Pattern:** Situational.
**Capital Rule:** Never decide in isolation. Change environment before deciding.

### 7. Lunar Authority
**Process:** Clarity unfolds over approximately one full lunar cycle (29 days).
**When Overridden:** Decisions feel unstable or premature.
**Timing Pattern:** Long cycle.
**Capital Rule:** Delay major capital decisions until full clarity emerges across a complete cycle.

## PART 3 — THE 12-MONTH WEALTH CYCLE

### Phase 1 — Strategic Planning (Months 1–3)
Review previous performance, identify new opportunities, design strategy.
Capital deployment: limited and exploratory.

### Phase 2 — Expansion Window (Months 4–7)
Momentum builds. New opportunities emerge, partnerships form, growth accelerates.
Highest probability window for scaling decisions.
Capital deployment: active, growth-oriented.

### Phase 3 — Consolidation (Months 8–10)
Strengthen systems, protect gains, optimise capital allocation.
Risk appetite reduces.
Capital deployment: selective and defensive.

### Phase 4 — Capital Protection (Months 11–12)
Markets move toward uncertainty. Review exposure, reduce risk, prepare for next cycle.
Capital deployment: minimal. Preserve and prepare.

## PART 5 — THE 7 CORE SIGNALS (Intelligence Layer)

These 7 signals convert Human Design and timing cycles into a practical decision intelligence system.

### Signal 1 — Clarity Signal
Measures decisional clarity vs confusion.
Green = high clarity, aligned authority, stable environment.
Red = conflicted inputs, overriding authority, external pressure distorting judgment.

### Signal 2 — Action Signal
Measures readiness to initiate or execute.
Green = authority aligned, timing supportive, energy confirmed.
Red = forcing, premature, energetic misalignment.

### Signal 3 — Expansion Signal
Measures growth-supportive timing.
Green = Jupiter influence active, expansion phase, momentum building.
Red = Saturn or Pluto pressure dominant, contraction period.

### Signal 4 — Pressure Signal
Measures internal or external compression and stress.
Green = low pressure environment, decisions made freely.
Red = urgency present, external forces compressing timeline, Saturn or Mars dominant.

### Signal 5 — Emotional Volatility Signal
Measures instability that can distort judgment.
Green = emotional wave at neutral, clarity window open.
Red = peak or trough of emotional wave, Mercury retrograde, lunar extremes.

### Signal 6 — Risk Signal
Measures likelihood of poor timing, overreach, or unnecessary exposure.
Green = risk is calculated and aligned with personal architecture.
Red = decision contradicts authority, timing not confirmed, environmental distortion present.

### Signal 7 — Opportunity Window Signal
Measures when timing conditions are most supportive for strategic moves.
Green = multiple signals aligned, personal cycle in expansion phase, macro timing supportive.
Amber = partial alignment, proceed with awareness.
Red = signals in conflict, hold and observe.

---

## HOW THE 7 SIGNALS PRODUCE GREEN / AMBER / RED

**GREEN WINDOW** — 5 or more signals showing Green.
Move with conviction. This is the optimal window for capital deployment, strategic decisions, and high-stakes action.

**AMBER WINDOW** — 3–4 signals showing Green.
Proceed with awareness. Calculated moves only. Validate before committing.

**RED WINDOW** — Fewer than 3 signals showing Green.
Hold. Protect capital. Observe. Prepare for the next Green window.
